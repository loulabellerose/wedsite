INFORMATION

TRANSPORT

Bus

The bus takes roughly 2 hours each way and return tickets usually cost around £16. There are a few companies to choose from but we recommend: Dublin Coach and Air Coach - we usually pick Dublin Coach as the buses are a little nicer and have a toilet on board, but the Air Coach buses run through the night if you aren't planning to stay over.

Train

Translink Enterprise run direct trains to Dublin from Central Station but it can be considerably more expensive than the bus and isn't any quicker. However, the Translink website runs web fare offers that start from £8.50 each way if booked 30 days in advance.


HOTELS *IMPORTANT*

Dublin is a host city for the 2020 UEFA European Championships and there happens to be a match in Dublin the night before our wedding. Hotels will get booked up quickly and prices are likely to increase so we HIGHLY RECOMMEND trying to sort out your accommodation as soon as possible. Booking.com offers the option to book now and pay on arrival which can be very useful. We plan to stay close to the venue but anywhere within a mile or so radius of Dublin City Centre would be ideal.


FOOD
